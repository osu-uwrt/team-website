Jaws

The 2013-2014 ROV, named Jaws, represents our move away from a traditional thruster configuration towards a low weight and low cost system. Jaws features two actuating thrusters near the dome controlled my internal servos with dynamic shaft-seals. This unique approach gives jaws total freedom to move in water while still allowing precision control via thrust vectoring algorithms.

Jaws was later reinvisioned and remade to better pursue what it was meant to do.

Components

Thrusters

Jaws - 2 was powered by three Seabotix BTD150 Thrusters. Using Sabertooth motor controllers, the vehicle was able to move in any direction and had plenty of power to drive it through the water.

Cameras

Jaws used generic off the shelf USB cameras. This was a cost saving measure. High performance machine vision cameras were not required for this application and therefore not purchased as they were for later vehicles.

Competiton

MATE 2014

Jaws was designed to compete in the 2014 Marine Advanced Technology Education International ROV Competition. Jaws never made it to the competition due to issues from using the new motor controllers. Due to a young and developing team just getting into advancing electronics, issues were not solved in time to make it to the final competition.
